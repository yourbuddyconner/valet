These pages are for teams deciding what work to hand Valet first. Pick the vertical closest to your team, and you leave with a set of plays you can put into production this week.

Each vertical is a playbook built from concrete **plays**. A play names a trigger (a webhook, a schedule, or someone asking), describes what Valet does step by step, lists the integrations involved, and calls out where an approval gate belongs. Every play maps to a [workflow](/product/workflows) you can copy: save it once, and it runs the same way every time instead of depending on how you phrase the request.

## How a vertical becomes reality

The path from a play on these pages to automation running in your org is the same every time:

1. **Connect the tools** the play uses — GitHub, Slack, Gmail, Linear, and the rest live in [integrations](/product/integrations/overview).
2. **Save the play as a workflow.** Build it in the visual editor, pick the trigger — webhook, schedule, or manual — and test a run. See [workflows](/product/workflows) and [triggers](/product/triggers).
3. **Set approvals** where the play touches something sensitive: merging code, sending email, changing customer records. A workflow pauses at the gate until a human approves, from web, Slack, or Telegram. See [approvals](/product/approvals).
4. **For organizations: set up a team.** A [team](/product/teams) owns the workflows, integrations, and memory for a vertical, and gives every member a shared team orchestrator to talk to in the web UI and bound Slack channels.

<Note>
Teams and team orchestrators are rolling out. Individual users can run every play with their personal orchestrator and workflows today.
</Note>
