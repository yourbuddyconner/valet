---
title: Access and Sharing
description: Decide who can use Valet, who can administer it, and who can see a task.
---

Valet needs clear access rules because it can see work details and use connected tools.

The product questions are simple:

- Who can sign in?
- Who can manage team settings?
- Who can see or join a task?
- Which connected tools can each person use?

## Signing in

Teams can limit who is allowed to join Valet. Common controls include approved domains, invites, and admin-managed access.

## Admins and members

Admins manage team-level setup:

- Access rules.
- Connected tools.
- Approval defaults.
- Shared workflows.
- Shared personas and skills.
- Channel settings.

Members use Valet for their own tasks and any shared work they are allowed to see.

## Sharing a task

Share a task when someone else needs to review, collaborate, or take over.

Use private tasks for:

- Personal memory.
- Sensitive customer information.
- Account setup.
- Work that only you should approve.

## Workspace access

Some tasks include a workspace with files, drafts, or engineering tools. A person should only open a workspace when they are allowed to inspect the task.

## Admin checklist

- Decide who can join.
- Choose who can be an admin.
- Decide what work is private by default.
- Decide when task sharing is allowed.
- Review connected tools and approvals regularly.
