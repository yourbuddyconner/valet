# Auth and Access

Valet separates three questions:

- Who can sign in?
- What can they administer?
- Which sessions and workspaces can they access?

This lets teams invite users, connect tools, and share work without making every session visible to everyone.

## Sign-in

Users sign in with supported identity providers such as GitHub or Google. Organization settings can restrict who is allowed to join.

Common access gates include:

- Approved email domains.
- Explicit invite lists.
- Email-specific invites.
- Admin-created users.
- Deployment-specific allowlists.

## Roles

Valet has normal users and admins.

Admins can manage organization-level settings such as integrations, provider keys, repositories, MCP connectors, and policy defaults. Normal users can work in sessions and connect personal tools according to the organization's configuration.

## Session access

Sessions can have owners, collaborators, and viewers.

| Role | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Owner | Controls the session and can resolve owner-scoped approvals. |
| Collaborator | Participates in the work. |
| Viewer | Can inspect the session without driving it. |

Share links and organization visibility settings can extend access when collaboration is needed.

## Workspace access

Some sessions expose additional workspace surfaces, such as development tools or task artifacts. Access to those surfaces should be short-lived and tied to session permissions.

Users should only open workspaces they are allowed to inspect.
