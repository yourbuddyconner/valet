Start with one real piece of work. Valet is easiest to understand when the task is clear, bounded, and easy to review.

## Before you start

- **A Valet account.** Open your organization's Valet web app URL (your admin has it) and sign in with Google, GitHub, or email — whichever your admin has enabled. If sign-ups are invite-based, use the invite link your admin sent.
- **At least one connected tool** for the task you have in mind (see step 2).

## Run your first task

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign in and look around">
    The sidebar is the whole product: **Dashboard**, **Orchestrator** (with its Chat and Memory), **Automation**, **Sessions**, **Integrations**, **Skills**, **Personas**, and **Settings**. Your orchestrator is your personal AI coworker — it has a name and handle (you chose them during onboarding), and the Dashboard shows a **Talk to \[name\]** button whenever you want it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect a tool">
    Go to **Integrations** in the sidebar and click **Connect Integration**. For Google, GitHub, Linear, and most services this opens the service's own sign-in screen — sign in, approve access, done. The card shows the connection status afterward.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Ask for a specific outcome">
    Open **Orchestrator → Chat** and make a bounded request: "Summarize the open Linear issues assigned to me and suggest the top three to handle." If Valet needs something it can't reach, it will say so — connect the missing tool or ask an admin to enable it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review before Valet acts">
    When Valet wants to send, write, delete, or change something important, an approval prompt appears in the chat (and in Slack or Telegram if connected). Read it, then approve once, allow for the session, or set a durable rule — see [approvals](/product/approvals).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use the result">
    Read the summary, inspect what changed, and send a follow-up if the task needs more work. Long-running work shows up under **Sessions** in the sidebar, where you can reopen it any time.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  The docs say "task" for a unit of work; the app's sidebar labels the list of them **Sessions**. Same thing — a session is one task's conversation, activity, and workspace.
</Note>

## Good first tasks

Good first tasks are easy to inspect:

- Summarize a set of issues, messages, or documents.
- Draft a follow-up plan from connected tools.
- Prepare a checklist for a recurring process.
- Research account details before a meeting.
- Triage a small queue and ask before writing updates.

## Save what worked

After a successful first task:

- Turn repeated steps into a [workflow](/product/workflows) — go to **Automation** and describe it; the copilot builds a first draft.
- Capture useful instructions as a [skill](/product/skills-and-personas).
- Connect more [tools](/product/tools) if Valet needed information it could not reach.
- Adjust [approvals](/product/approvals) if the task prompted too often or too little.
