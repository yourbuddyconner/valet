Google Workspace is where a lot of real work lives, and this integration gives Valet hands there: it can draft and revise Google Docs, read and build spreadsheets, and find, copy, and organize Drive files. Read this page to learn what Valet can do in each app, how access is controlled, and the habits that get you good results.

Typical asks look like:

- "Turn my meeting notes doc into a client-ready proposal."
- "Append this week's numbers to the tracking sheet and update the chart."
- "Find last quarter's planning docs and summarize the open decisions."
- "Create the onboarding checklist from our template, filled in for the new hire."

## What Valet can do

**Docs.** Valet reads documents as text, markdown, or a structured form with character indices, so it can make surgical edits — insert or replace a specific passage — as well as append whole formatted sections written in markdown. It handles structure (tables, images, page and section breaks, tabs), formatting (character and paragraph styles, page orientation), and the full comment lifecycle: list, add, reply, resolve, delete.

**Sheets.** Valet reads and writes ranges, appends rows, and batches writes across ranges. Beyond data, it does real spreadsheet construction: cell and border formatting, conditional formatting, frozen headers, column sizing, dropdown validation, protected ranges, named tables, and charts. "Build me a tracker with a summary tab and a chart" is one ask, not ten.

**Drive.** Valet lists, searches, and downloads files; creates documents and folders; copies, moves, and renames; and creates documents from a template with placeholder substitution — the workhorse for "generate one of these per customer" workflows.

## Setup

Valet uses standard Google OAuth. You connect your own Google account from Valet's connection screen, and Valet acts as that account when it makes API calls — it does not impersonate other users or share one account across the team. See [OAuth setup](/oauth-setup) for the environment variables and connection flow.

Three consequences worth internalizing:

- Valet only sees what your own Google account can see.
- Permissions are governed by Google's normal sharing model.
- Revoking access at `myaccount.google.com` revokes it for Valet immediately.

## What Valet can reach

Your account's permissions are the outer boundary. Inside that boundary, your admin can enable a **labels-based access guard** that narrows what Valet may touch:

1. The admin defines one or more Google Drive labels that mark files as available to Valet.
2. Before Valet reads, lists, modifies, or copies a file, it checks for one of those labels.
3. Files without a required label behave as if they do not exist — Valet cannot list, search, or open them.
4. Files Valet creates get the label automatically, so follow-up access works.

If the guard is not enabled, Valet falls back to standard Google permissions: anything your account can see, Valet can see. Either way the rule of thumb is: a file is touchable when your account can see it **and** (if the guard is on) it has the required label.

<Note>
  Search and listing follow the same rules. A doc you expect in the results may be missing because it isn't labeled — even though Valet could open it directly if you pasted its URL and it carried the label. If Valet says it can't find a file you know exists, check the label before anything else.
</Note>

## Getting good results

<Tip>
  The single most reliable way to point Valet at a file is to paste its Docs, Sheets, or Drive URL into the task. Valet pulls the file ID from the URL and works from there — no ambiguous searches, no listing surprises.
</Tip>

A few behaviors catch people off guard. Knowing them up front is most of the battle:

**"Copy and reformat" can become a rewrite.** When you ask Valet to produce a reformatted version of a doc, the most reliable path is often generating a new document with the desired structure — less like Google's "Make a copy," more like a regenerated document. If preserving the original's revision history, comments, or sharing matters, say so: "make a Drive copy first, then edit the copy."

**Formatting fidelity is not perfect.** Valet reads and writes a lot of rich formatting, but exotic layouts, embedded charts, and complex tables may not round-trip exactly. For high-stakes documents, have Valet do the content work and do the final polish yourself.

**Comment cleanup is real cleanup.** Valet can resolve and delete comments, and the result looks exactly like a manual resolve — the thread leaves the doc's main view. Be deliberate when asking it to tidy comment threads.

## Who owns what

Some operations create files; others modify files you already own. The distinction matters when you go looking for the result:

| Operation | Who owns the result | The original |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Create a new doc or sheet | Your connected Google account | No original — fresh file |
| Copy a file | The account the copy is made for | Untouched |
| Edit, append, format, or rename your file | You (unchanged) | Modified in place |

Files Valet creates land in the Drive of the connected account. If you want them in a specific folder or shared drive, say where — or ask Valet to move them after.

## Approvals

Reads are low risk and run freely. Edits — inserting text, writing ranges, formatting, creating files — are medium risk and follow your [approval settings](/product/approvals). Destructive operations are high risk and prompt by default: deleting files or content ranges, replacing an entire document body, deleting sheets, tables, or comment threads. The [action reference](#action-reference) below lists the risk level per action.

## Gmail and Google Calendar

[Gmail](/product/integrations/gmail) and [Google Calendar](/product/integrations/google-calendar) are separate integrations that use the same per-user OAuth model: Valet acts as your account and sees your inbox and calendar, not the team's. The same habit applies — paste the thread or event link when you mean a specific one.

## Disconnecting

Disconnect at any time, from either side:

- Inside Valet, remove the Google integration.
- At Google, revoke Valet's access at `myaccount.google.com` under "Third-party apps with account access."

Either action immediately stops new calls on your behalf. Past activity — files created, edits made — is not undone.
